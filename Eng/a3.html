Chapter 2: The Root of Chaos

Where the nation’s treasury was swallowed into personal pockets instead of bringing progress to the villages and granting freedom, it led only to division.
Before long, Manuel too began creating works that emphasized the importance of education and the rights of Filipinos. His pieces gained recognition and were widely embraced.
Manuel carried on this mission for the youth, fully aware that he might clash with the high officials of the government—men who could, without hesitation, order his arrest.
There was Emilio Dela Cruz, a politician infamous for his hypocrisy and arrogance, together with his right-hand man Hernando Zalasar, who was willing to kill for money.
A gathering was held in Intramuros for the principalia, politicians, and merchants. The family of Felecita was invited, as was Manuel, who was asked to perform a short play.
Manuel stood on stage and portrayed the value of education for the youth—children born into poverty, forced to labor, and by the age of thirty-four still unable to read or write. He went on to criticize the present government, which enraged Emilio. Furious, Emilio went up to the stage and declared:
“E: What does a poor man like you know about politics? Go back to plowing the fields, and stop meddling in matters you cannot comprehend.”
