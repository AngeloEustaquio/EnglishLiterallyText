Many people in the world say the phrase “history repeats itself,” but if we observe closely, history teaches us to correct our wrong behaviors—behaviors that are still ongoing in the present. For example, Hernando’s murder of Manuel’s parents for money—no amount of money can ever equal the value of a human life—and corruption, denial of rights, and selfish interests. These problems existed in the past, but if we do not learn from them, we will not be able to change the present. We, as people, are also the reason why the events of the past continue to happen.

The work Libertas pro Indio is based on my own concept. The characters and events are fictional, but the setting is inspired by real life, such as the University of Santo Tomas, Intramuros, and others.
