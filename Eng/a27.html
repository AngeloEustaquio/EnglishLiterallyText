Personal Reflection
In the story of Manuel, who battles against evil, the problem he faced is not only an issue of the past but continues even in the present. The past stands as a witness to give us knowledge of what is right to do, yet it is we ourselves who fail to uphold it. Many people in the world say the phrase “History repeats itself”—but if we truly observe, history teaches us to correct the wrong practices. It is only we, living in the present, who choose to continue them. For example, Hernando’s killing of Manuel’s parents in exchange for money—no amount of wealth can ever equal a person’s life. Corruption, the denial of rights, and self-interest are also among these problems. These issues have existed even long ago, but if we do not learn, we will never be able to change the present. We, the people, are the very reason why the wrongs of the past continue to happen today.

The work Libertas pro Indio is based on my own concept. The characters and events are conceptual, but the places are drawn from real life, such as the University of Santo Tomas, Intramuros, and others.
