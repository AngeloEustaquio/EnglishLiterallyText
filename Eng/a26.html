After a week, Manuel recovered thanks to the care of his wife.
Manuel was appointed as the new governor, replacing Emilio... His first orders were to give the poor the money of the town that they deserved to live comfortably, provide jobs for the Indios, and give scholarships to children who could not afford education.
Meanwhile, Juan and Andres continued creating their works for future generations.
Soon, Manila also prospered due to proper use of funds... Many children were able to read and write.
Because of the positive changes and new hope, Manuel and Felecita decided to remarry, this time inside the capitol... Felecita invited her parents to attend, and Don Fernando and Doña Juana forgave Manuel for everything he did to Felecita.
They realized that the wishes of their child were more important than their own interests.
Eventually, Manuel and Felecita were blessed with two children, named Antonio and Maria, to honor Manuel’s deceased parents. They lived happily with this new hope.
