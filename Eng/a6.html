Manuel was unaware that Gustavo was the son of Emilio. In their first meeting, Gustavo introduced himself as a poet from Cebu, gentle and meek like a lamb. Because of this, Manuel felt an instant closeness to him.
They began preparing for the league, and Manuel composed his first poem that would introduce him not only to Intramuros but to all of Manila—his piece titled “Voice of the Poor.”
Voice of the Poor
“ City of Intramuros, crumbling little by little... ”
Gustavo noticed that Manuel’s rhyme and verses were far more captivating than his own. Out of envy, he attempted to swap their works, but he failed. Manuel clutched his poem tightly, determined that it be recited not only for the league, but also as the true voice of the Filipino people.
Two weeks later, the two poets were ready to perform. Gustavo would recite first, followed by Manuel. Their verses would depict the rights of the Filipinos.
“ G: Amigo, are you ready for the recital? I know we share the same goal—together we shall reach what we dream of and win the prize money. ”
“ M: Por favor, amigo, but money is not the reason I am here. I stand here to give voice to the poor who have none. ”
