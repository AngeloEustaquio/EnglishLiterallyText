“Applause!” W: A brilliant poem from Gustavo. Now we will hear the next poet, known as Kiko, a master in the art of writing, Manuel Ibarra. “Applause!”
“ M: Well, ladies and gentlemen, I dedicate this poem to the poor who have no right to education.”
Tinig ng Pobre
Manuel Ibarra
Intramuros, my town,
Slowly decaying it seems,
Money meant for education, how the governor pockets it.
Is it right or proper?
Wealth shines for the rich,
Yet the poor continue to suffer.
“ W: A joyful and moving poem from Manuel, truly captivating and full of meaning for the Filipino people.”
"Applause!"
“ W: Now we shall listen to the poets from the University of Ateneo de Manila.”
Juan Bonifacio and Andres Luna, famous poets from the University of Ateneo de Manila, Indios who seek change, shared Manuel’s ideals, and thus became his close friends.
