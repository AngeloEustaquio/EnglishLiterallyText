“ G: If that is what you wish, amigo, but from my perspective, money is far more important than freedom. The Indios are not worthy of education. Besides, you gain nothing from what you do. My advice is to abandon it altogether. ”
“ M: I cannot agree with you. Education is the right of every Filipino, whether poor or wealthy. It seems we shall not find common ground here. ”
“ G: So be it. But if you stand in my way, I will not hesitate to end this friendship. ”
“ M: If that is what you desire. ”
The day of the contest arrived—a day long awaited by many. Among the audience were the Insulares, the Principalia, the Ilustrados, and even the governor himself.
“ W: Buenos días, señores y señoras. We are here to offer you a poem that surely will leave a lesson to be remembered. ”
“ W: Bueno, let us begin the league. ”
Fangs Upon the Nation
Gustavo Dela Cruz
Oh beloved land,
The poor who have no rights,
They must be given their due.
Where does the nation’s wealth go?
Rights are needed—
The poor without a voice, I shall rise.
