Manuel's plan was to start the poem by highlighting the evil deeds of Emilio and Gustavo… Then Juan's group would attack the guards outside the hall, and afterwards Andres’ group would confront Emilio and Gustavo to punish them for their wrongdoing.
March 17, 1897—the long-awaited day. Juan and Andres’ group were ready… as well as the poem that would end the cruelty of the father and son.
At dusk, the hall event began… The music played and the guests danced… while Emilio and Gustavo enjoyed the company of the women.
Hernando went outside the hall to keep watch, where Juan spotted him and covered Hernando’s head with a sack… tied him up and took his cargo.
Andres then captured the poet who was to recite at the hall, so that Manuel could deliver the poem instead.
All Manuel needed was a signal to confront Emilio and Gustavo.
Manuel was dressed in armor and a mask to conceal his identity from Emilio and Gustavo.
“M: Good evening, dear guests.”
“M: I am Kiko, and I will present to you an elegant poem I wrote for you.”
“K: This poem is titled ‘In the Shadow of Greed.’”
