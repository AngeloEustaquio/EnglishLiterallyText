Manuel noticed Felecita sitting alone, so he approached her.
“ F: Oh, Manuel, it's you. Please, have a seat.”
“ M: Thank you, Miss Felecita.”
“ F: Do not call me ‘Miss’ anymore, you rascal, or I will report you to the civil guards.”
“ M: Hoo...”
“ F: What I mean is you don’t have to call me ‘Miss’; just call me Felecita.”
“ M: Isn’t that improper? You are a Principalia and I am just an Indio.”
“ F: I don’t care! We are all creations of God, equal whether rich or poor. As you said, rich or poor, everyone has rights.”
“ M: You are different from other people here; you understand poor people like me.”
“ F: Uh, you know, I like you.”
“ M: You like me?”
“ F: I mean I like what you do, your personality, and how you give a voice to the Indios.”
“ F: Oh, by the way, I didn’t properly introduce myself. I am Felecita Trinidad del Buente Deguzman, from Kawit, Cavite. I studied Embroidery in Spain, and I am currently studying Grammar.”
