“M: I may be but a poor man thirsting for change for the youth, and though my knowledge of politics may be lacking, I know this: every person has the right to reach their aspirations. Whether rich or poor, one’s station in life should never be a barrier to education. If only the nation’s wealth were used properly, this land would already be filled with scholars.”
The crowd praised Manuel for his conviction, while Emilio, consumed by shame, quickly departed with his guards.
Felicita noticed Manuel’s gift for the stage, and the strength of his spirit as he gave voice to those who had none.
Don Fernando approached Manuel with admiration. He offered him work—to compose verses for his wife’s upcoming birthday. It was then that Don Fernando introduced his only daughter, Felicita.
Manuel could scarcely grasp what was unfolding; his mind wandered as if lost in a dream. Felicita’s eyes glimmered like stars shining across the heavens, casting light upon the shadows of his thoughts. Time itself seemed to pause, and his heartbeat quickened, as though a storm had begun to rage within his very soul.
