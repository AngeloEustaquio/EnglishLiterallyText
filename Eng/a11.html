Chapter 3: Friendship and Adventures

“ A: Juan and I have observed your works and it seems we share the same goal—a change for the Filipinos.”
“ M: I am well. Yes, it is true, because so many elders still cannot write or read. So many Indios are not allowed to study, and there are so many reasons for this.”
“ J: By the way, my name is Juan Luna, I am from Tondo.”
“ A: I am Andres Luna, from the city of Balintawak. Juan and I are friends, looking for people who can join us in the change we advocate. If you wish, you can join our group.”
“ M: I accept your offer. I am Manuel Ibarra from Bagumbayan, and this is my friend …”
“ G: Enough, I do not care about the changes you promote. Do as you please, and from now on let us forget our friendship, Manuel.”
“ M: Friend, …”
“ A: Leave him be. I do not like his behavior, especially when he glances around. I know he is greedy, and we cannot trust him.”
“ J: Well, from today, you are a member of our group. We will be the voice of the poor Filipinos.”
“ M: Thank you very much, my new friends. Now I know I have companions for my goals, and surely we will achieve our high dreams for the nation.”
